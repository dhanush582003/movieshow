Loki

The series begins after Avengers: Endgame, which saw Loki steal the Tesseract during the 2012 events of The Avengers (2012),[72] creating an alternate timeline from the main MCU films.[73] The first season sees the Loki time variant traveling through time and altering human history,[2][9] with a "man-on-the-run" and an "unexpected" science fiction quality to it;[74][75] the season also explores Loki's identity.[76][77] Loki falls in love with his female variant, Sylvie, in the season, which was a large part of Waldron's pitch for the series. He noted they were uncertain if portraying Loki falling in love with another version of himself was "too crazy". He continued by saying Loki was "ultimately about self-love, self-reflection, and forgiving yourself" and it "felt right" for the series be the character's first "real love story".
